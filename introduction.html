Introduction
            Modern information and communication technologies, such as mobile technologies and Quick Response (QR) codes, have great potential to improve teaching and learning because mobile technologies enable learning across multiple contexts, through content interactions. In other words, learners can learn anytime and anywhere and learning can be personalized, customized and authentic.
QR codes have become widely popular because they provide a large amount of data comparatively quickly. QR codes can contain information such as text, URL links, or other data that can direct users to sources of further information about a particular place or subject
              QR codes can meet the needs of learners in a range of ways. First, it is possible to enrich paper-based materials to serve different types of learners. For instance, QR codes in paper-based tasks can contain links to multimedia resources such as audio materials or video clips.
             QR codes can guide learners through the self-assessment process.The QR code printed on the worksheet directly linked to a web page with the right answers and guided the learners through the individual exercises.
QR technology provides a wealth of benefits for the techno-friendly teachers. Besides the fact that most students own mobile phones that can read QR codes in seconds, they also often have access to laptops in school. Students are likely to get excited at the prospect of opening at the click of a button!
QR codes are brilliant in education:
  1. Inconspicuous as they may seem, QR codes can hold over 4000 characters of information. Instead of printing out a long web article to students, QR codes could give them direct and instantaneous access to the same resource in class or at home. It saves time.
  2. They are very easy to generate. To create a QR code, we only need to copy and paste the address of a web page into QR generating software. Code once produces  can then be transferred to teaching resource
  3. QR codes can be printed on virtually anything: paper, textile, walls, even on your skin as a tattoo for those who are willing to show total commitment to their subject (!).
  4. Some schools use them in the reception area to provide extra information about school events to students, parents and visitors.
     QR codes are becoming a popular tool in modern education.  They combine speed, ease and novelty with the capacity to hold a large amount of data.
