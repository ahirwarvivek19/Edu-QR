Class  12th   Practicals

Biology

Study of Pollen Germination

Our aim is to study pollen germination on a slide

Studies on Turbidity, pH and Microbial Presence in Water

Our aim is to study different water bodies for pH, clarity and presence of any living organisms

Study of Physical Properties of Soil

Our aim is to collect and study soil for texture, moisture content, pH and water holding capacity.

Study of pollutants in Air

To study the presence of suspended particulate matter in the air at different sites.

Study of plant population density by quadrat method

Our aim is to study plant population density by quadrat method.

Study of plant population frequency by quadrat method

Our aim is to study plant population frequency by quadrat method.

Study Mitosis in Onion Root Tip

Our aim is to prepare a temporary mount of onion root tip to study mitosis.

Action of Salivary Amylase on Starch

Our aim is to study the effect of different temperatures and pH on the activity of salivary content, amylase on starch.

Physics

Metre Bridge-Resistance of a wire

To find the resistance of a given wire using a metre bridge and hence determine the specific resistance of its materials.

Ohm's law and resistance

 To determine the resistance per cm of a given wire by plotting a graph of potential difference versus current, and hence to determine its resistivity.

Metre bridge- Law of combination of resistors

To verify the laws of combination (series/parallel) of resistances using a metre bridge.

The potentiometer- Comparison of emf

To compare the emf ’s of two given primary cells using potentiometer.

The Potentiometer-Internal resistance of a cell

To determine the internal resistance of given primary cell using potentiometer

Figure of Merit of a galvanometer

To determine resistance of a galvanometer by half-deflection method and to find its figure of
merit

AC Sonometer

To find the frequency of the ac mains with a sonometer

The Concave mirror - u-v method

To find the value of v for different values of u in case of a concave mirror and to find the focal
length.

Convex Mirror-Focal Length

To find the focal length of a convex mirror, using a convex lens.

Concave Lens - Focal Length

To find the focal length of a concave lens, using a convex lens.

Refraction through a Prism

To determine angle of minimum deviation for a given prism by plotting a graph between the angle
of incidence and the angle of deviation.

Refractive Index of Liquid

To find refractive index of a liquid by using (i) concave mirror, (ii) convex lens and plane mirror.

Diode characteristics

To draw the I-V characteristics curves of a p-n junction in forward bias and reverse bias

Zener Diode

To draw the characteristics curve of a zener diode and to determine its reverse break down
voltage

Transistor characteristics

To study the characteristics of a common-emitter npn or pnp transistor and to find out the values
of current and voltage gains

Conversion of Galvanometer to Ammeter

To convert the given galvanometer (of known resistance of figure of merit) into an ammeter and
voltmeter of desired range and to verify the same.

Chemistry

Preparation of Lyophilic and Lyophobic Sols

Surface Chemistry
(a) Preparation of one lyophilic and one lyophobic sol.
Lyophilic sol: starch, egg albumin and gum.
Lyophobic sol: aluminium hydroxide, ferric hydroxide, arsenious sulphide.
(b) Dialysis of sol prepared in (a) above.
(c) Study of the role of emulsifying agent in stabilizing the emulsions of different oils.

Kinetic Study on the Reaction between Iodide Ions and Hydrogen Peroxide

Chemical Kinetics
(a) Effect of concentration and temperature on the rate of reaction between sodium thiosulphate
and hydrochloric acid .
(b) Study of reaction rates of any one of the following:
(i) Reaction of iodide ion with hydrogen peroxide at room temperature
using different concentrations of iodide ions.
(ii) Reaction between potassium iodate (KIO3) and sodium sulphite (Na2SO3) using starch
solution as indicator (clock reaction).

Thermochemistry

Thermochemistry
Any one of the following experiments :
(a) Enthalpy of dissolution of copper sulphate or potassium nitrate.
(b) Enthalpy of neutralization of strong acid (HCl) and strong base (NaOH)
(c) Determination of enthalpy change during interaction (Hydrogen bond formation) between acetone
and chloroform.

Determination of Emf of a Cell

Electrochemistry
Variation of cell potential in Zn/Zn2+//Cu2+/Cu with change in concentration of electrolytes (CuSO4 or
ZnSO4) at room temperature.

Paper Chromatography

Chromatography
(a) Separation of pigments from extracts of leaves and flowers by paper chromatography and
determination of Rf values.
(b) Separation of constituents present in an inorganic mixture containing two cations only (constituents
having wide difference in Rf values to be provided).

Preparation of Inorganic Compounds

Preparation of Inorganic Compounds
(a) Preparation of double salt of ferrous ammonium sulphate or potash alum.
(b) Preparation of potassium ferric oxalate.

Preparation of Organic Compounds

Preparation of Organic Compounds
Preparation of any one of the following compounds:
(a) Acetanilide
(b) Di-benzal acetone
(c) p-Nitroacetanilide
(d) Aniline yellow or 2 - Napththol aniline dye

Tests for the Functional Group Present in the Organic Compounds

Test for the Functional Groups Present in Organic Compounds
Unsaturation, alcoholic, phenolic, aldehydic, ketonic, carboxylic and amino (primary) groups.

Qualitative Analysis of Carbohydrates

Characteristic Tests of Carbohydrates, Fats and Proteins in Pure Samples and Their
Detection in Given Food Stuffs.

Determination of Concentration of KMnO4 Solution Using Oxalic Acid

Determination of Concentration/Molarity of KMnO4 Solution by Titrating it against a
Standard Solution of –
(i) Oxalic acid
(ii) Ferrous ammonium sulphate
